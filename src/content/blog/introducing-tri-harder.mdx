## What is TRI-HARDER?

TRI-HARDER is a free AI triathlon coaching app that connects to your training data — from Strava or intervals.icu — and provides personalised coaching analysis, training plans, and downloadable reports.

Unlike generic training plan generators, TRI-HARDER actually *understands* your training. It knows your recent workouts, your race goals, your injury history, and your fitness metrics. When you ask it a question, it answers with context — not boilerplate.

## Why build another coaching tool?

Most triathlon coaching falls into two camps: expensive human coaching or rigid plan generators that don't adapt to what's actually happening in your training.

TRI-HARDER sits in between. It's a **conversational AI coach** that analyses your real data and gives you specific, contextual guidance. It remembers your goals and preferences across sessions, tracks your training load metrics (CTL, ATL, TSB), and can generate comprehensive coaching reports you can download as PDF.

## What can it do?

Here's a quick overview of the key features:

- **AI Chat Coach** — have natural conversations about your training, grounded in expert triathlon coaching knowledge from Joe Friel's *Triathlete's Training Bible*
- **Training Dashboard** — visualise your fitness, fatigue, and form with CTL/ATL/TSB charts and workload ratios
- **Coaching Reports** — generate downloadable PDF reports with AI-powered progress analysis
- **Training Calendar** — plan and track workouts with RPE ratings and completion notes
- **Coaching Memory** — the AI remembers your goals, injuries, and preferences across sessions

## Privacy-first design

Your training data stays private. The AI model key is kept server-side, there are no third-party analytics, and you control what gets stored. PDF reports are generated entirely in your browser — no data leaves your device.

## How to get started

1. Visit the TRI-HARDER app
2. Connect your Strava or intervals.icu account
3. Start chatting with your AI coach

It's free, privacy-first, and built by a triathlete who thinks CTL/ATL charts are genuinely exciting.
